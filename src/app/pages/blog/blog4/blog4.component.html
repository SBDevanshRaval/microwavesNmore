Top 10 Must-Have Kitchen Appliances for New Homeowners in the GTA

Congratulations on your new home in the Greater Toronto Area (GTA)! Whether you’ve moved into a downtown Toronto condo or a family home in Richmond Hill or Whitby, one thing’s certain — your kitchen is about to become the heart of your home.

Here’s your ultimate checklist of the Top 10 Must-Have Kitchen Appliances to get your culinary journey started.

1. Microwave Oven

An absolute essential. From reheating leftovers to popping popcorn or defrosting meat, a microwave is your everyday lifesaver.

Pro Tip: Go for a convection model if you want baking features without a full oven.

Explore microwave ovens at MicrowavesnMore.in

2. Air Fryer

Crispy food without the guilt? Yes, please. Air fryers are a GTA kitchen favorite for health-conscious and fast cooking.

3. Blender

From smoothies to soups, a blender is essential. Look for multiple speeds and a durable, easy-to-clean design.

4. Toaster or Toaster Oven

Whether it’s bagels or pizza slices, a 2-slice toaster or toaster oven is a versatile must-have. Bonus: toaster ovens can bake too!

5. Coffee Maker

Drip coffee or pod-based? Either way, a reliable coffee maker is a Canadian kitchen staple. Look for Keurig or Hamilton Beach models.

6. Rice Cooker / Multi-Cooker (Instant Pot)

Perfect for cooking rice, lentils, steaming veggies, and more. Great for multicultural cooking in GTA homes.

7. Food Processor

Chop, shred, knead, and more. Saves time and effort in meal prep. Look for multiple attachments.

8. Electric Kettle

Hot drinks, instant noodles, boiling water — an electric kettle with auto shut-off is a must for winter in Canada.

9. Hand Mixer / Stand Mixer

If you bake, this is essential. Hand mixers are affordable while stand mixers are great for heavy-duty tasks.

Check for KitchenAid or Cuisinart deals at MicrowavesnMore.in

10. Vacuum Cleaner (Yes, for the Kitchen Too!)

Crumbs, dust, and daily mess — a compact cordless vacuum makes kitchen cleaning quick and easy.

Final Checklist for GTA New Homeowners:

  • Prioritize versatile appliances
  • Look for energy-efficient models
  • Shop local liquidation or refurbished suppliers
  • Measure your counter space before buying
